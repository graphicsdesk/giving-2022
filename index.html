Columbia Athletics brings in over $3 million on Giving Day

By Jorge Hernandez

November 4, 2022

Photo by Caitlin Buckley

During last week’s annual Giving Day, the University amassed just under $30 million in donations. Following trends in previous years, the three causes that received the most donations overall were Columbia College, Columbia Athletics, and Barnard College.

Columbia Athletics, which consistently receives some of the most donations on Giving Day, garnered $3,369,021.93 on Giving Day this year. This marks an almost 15 percent increase in donations from the previous year—more than enough to compensate for the 7.9 percent decrease Athletics saw in donations in 2021. The three sports that received the most donations were football, men’s golf, and women’s golf. Last year, football faced a sharp decline in donations, only receiving about $180,000, falling from its usual first place finish on Giving Day. Breaking from last year’s abnormality, football reclaimed its spot on top with a sharp 73.3 percent increase, blowing its 2019, 2020, and 2021 totals out of the water with $312,859 in donations. Despite good fortune on Giving Day, Columbia football has not been as lucky on the field. The team sits in last place in the Ivy League standings and has yet to win a single conference matchup, losing by an average of 16.5 points to each Ancient Eight opponent.

Men’s and women’s golf received the second and third most donations, respectively. Men’s golf accrued $157,641, while their female counterparts came just behind them raising $142,468. Potentially aided by their second place finish in the 2022 Ivy League Men’s Golf Championship, men’s golf experienced a 62 percent increase in donations from 2021. The women’s team, which also placed second in the Ivy League Golf Championship, experienced one of the sharpest increases in donations. Last year, they received the second lowest amount of donations, a mere $22,801. This signals a meteoric 524.8 percent increase in donation amount, which will surely aid the team.

Other notable sports which received hefty donation amounts were fencing, men’s swimming, and women’s and men’s basketball.

Fencing, one of the few sports at Columbia that is consistently competitive for both an Ivy League and national title, received $120,394.86 in donations. Despite an overall third place finish and a national title from senior Ashton Daniel at NCAA Championships last season, the team faced a stark 13.2 percent decrease in overall donations. This number stands in even deeper contrast to the $201,052.06 they received in 2019.

Men’s swimming also benefited from an increase in donations. This year, it accumulated $114,824.07 in donations, as compared to its previous total of $84,205.14, demonstrating a 36.4 percent increase. This comes after senior diver Jonathan Suckow’s standout first-place finish at the Ivy League Championships last year.

Men’s basketball, which for the last two seasons has only won two of its 26 Ivy League matches, also saw a boost in donations, despite the lack of success on the court. It received $106,492.77, marking a 14.8 percent increase from their previous total of $92,783. However, the team still stands well below its 2019 donation amount of $250,715. Women’s basketball also saw an increase in donations, albeit a smaller one than its male counterpart. At the conclusion of Giving Day, Columbia Athletics expressed its appreciation for the generosity of its donors.

"We cannot say thank you enough to everyone who supported Columbia Athletics on Giving Day," Athletic Director Peter Pilling said in a press release.

"Each year, it is amazing to see how everyone rallies together with the common goal of enhancing the student-athlete experiences. The level of generosity from our alumni and friends of our sport programs and department is what makes Columbia a truly special place."
